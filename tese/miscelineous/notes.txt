¶1 — DATA GROWTH (2-3 sentences)
  "In 2025, global internet traffic exceeded 8,800 exabytes — more than double
   the volume recorded in 2020 [chart citation]. Mobile traffic alone nearly tripled
   over the same period, driven by streaming, cloud services, and proliferating
   connected devices."
  
  → Immediately establishes scale and urgency.

¶2 — CLOUD (3-4 sentences)
  "Cloud computing has absorbed this growth through elastic provisioning,
   pay-as-you-go pricing, and global Points of Presence. Hyperscale data centers
   process and store the bulk of this traffic..."
  
  → Cloud as the solution to data volume.

¶3 — CLOUD LIMITATIONS → EDGE (3-4 sentences)
  "...But centralized cloud architectures impose structural latency: a user
   in Singapore accessing a data center in Virginia pays 250+ ms Round-Trip Time
   regardless of provisioning scale. Edge computing addresses this by placing
   compute and storage at the network periphery — closer to where data is
   generated and consumed."
  
  → Edge as the logical next step.

¶4 — STANDARD EDGE PLATFORM ARCHITECTURE (4-5 sentences)
  "Edge platforms deliver services through three functions: monitoring
   infrastructure health, load balancing traffic across backend instances,
   and auto-scaling resources in response to demand shifts. In every major
   architecture — Kubernetes, NFV MANO frameworks, MEC platforms — these three
   are implemented as separate components with independent control loops."
  
  → Introduce the object of study.

¶5 — THE COORDINATION GAP (4-5 sentences)
  "Each handoff between components introduces delay. A Prometheus scrape
   interval, an AlertManager evaluation, an HPA reconciliation, and a kube-proxy
   endpoint propagation can accumulate 30–120 seconds of coordination latency —
   even though the container itself boots in 10 seconds. This phenomenon has been
   documented, observed as a side effect, and called for — but never isolated
   or measured as an independent experimental variable."
  
  → The gap.

¶6 — THIS THESIS (4-5 sentences)
  "This thesis experimentally characterizes the coordination gap by decomposing
   it into three independently testable links: how overload is detected (trigger
   quality), how fast that information reaches the controller (delivery cadence),
   and how quickly new capacity receives traffic (routing awareness). Using a
   stateful edge workload — a content-discovery platform backed by a
   network-partitioned document database — and a unified SDN controller as the
   experimental apparatus, we isolate each link, vary it independently, and
   reconstruct the compound coordination tax that every separated architecture
   pays."
